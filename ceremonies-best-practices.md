Scrum defines five timeboxed events. Each serves a specific purpose. The difference between effective and ineffective ceremonies comes down to two best practices.

---

## What Ceremonies Actually Are

Scrum ceremonies aren't status reports or presentation slots. They're **timeboxed collaboration events** built on the three pillars of empiricism:

- **Transparency** — Everyone sees the real state of the work
- **Inspection** — Frequent, lightweight checks against the goal
- **Adaptation** — Immediate course correction based on what you find

Each event has a clear purpose, a fixed duration, and a concrete output. If yours don't, they're meetings wearing Scrum clothes.

---

## The Five Events at a Glance

| Event | Timebox | Purpose | Output |
|-------|---------|---------|--------|
| **Sprint Planning** | 4-8 hrs | Define Sprint Goal, select Backlog items | Sprint Backlog + Sprint Goal |
| **Daily Scrum** | 15 min | Sync on progress, plan next 24h | Updated plan |
| **Sprint Review** | 2-4 hrs | Demo Increment, gather feedback | Revised Product Backlog |
| **Sprint Retro** | 1.5-3 hrs | Inspect process, plan improvements | One actionable improvement |
| **Backlog Refinement** | 10% capacity | Refine items for future Sprints | Prioritized, estimated Backlog |

---

## Best Practice #1: Strict Timeboxing + Clear Goals

Every ceremony needs a timer and a stated goal before it starts. No exceptions.

**Sprint Planning** — The Product Owner states the Product Goal. The team answers: *What? How? Why?* If the "why" isn't clear, keep planning. Without a Sprint Goal, you're just scheduling work.

**Daily Scrum** — 15 minutes. Developers only. Three questions rephrased: *What did we do toward the Sprint Goal? What will we do today? Any blockers?* That's it. No scroll-through-the-board. No problem-solving mid-meeting.

**Sprint Review** — A working session, not a presentation. Stakeholders interact with the Increment. Feedback goes straight into the Product Backlog. If you're presenting slides, you're doing it wrong.

**Sprint Retro** — The one event where no stakeholders are invited. It's the team's safe space to inspect how they work together. Blameless, honest, and actionable.

**Backlog Refinement** — Ongoing. The team spends ~10% of Sprint capacity breaking down large items, estimating, and reordering. Don't cram it into one session; weave it through the Sprint.

---

## Best Practice #2: Action-Oriented Outcomes

A ceremony without a tangible output is a meeting.

| Event | Must Produce |
|-------|-------------|
| Sprint Planning | Sprint Goal + Sprint Backlog items |
| Daily Scrum | Updated plan for the next 24 hours |
| Sprint Review | Revised Product Backlog with new priorities |
| Sprint Retro | One improvement with an owner and a deadline |
| Backlog Refinement | Sized and ordered Backlog items |

If someone asks "what did we get from that ceremony?" and the answer is vague, the ceremony failed.

---

## The Two Best Practices Together

Strict timeboxing without clear goals = fast, pointless meetings. Clear goals without timeboxes = productive drift into the next meeting slot.

| | No Timebox | Strict Timebox |
|--|-----------|----------------|
| **Vague Goal** | Endless venting | Fast venting |
| **Clear Goal** | Good but bloated | Lean and productive |

The magic lives in the bottom-right quadrant: **clear goal + strict timebox**.

---

## The One-Liner

Timebox the input, define the output, and your ceremonies will stop wasting everyone's time.
